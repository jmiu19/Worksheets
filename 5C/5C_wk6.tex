In order to make electron flow in a conductor to create a current, we need to apply a electric potential difference across the two ends of the conductor. Let us call the potential difference across the two ends as $V$, and the resulting current as $I$, we have the famous Ohm's Law, \footnote{You can think of this in this way: Imagine a cylindrical conductor. The top circular disk of it is positively charged and the bottom disk is negatively charged. Then electrons in the cylinder will want to move upwards towards the top disk due to the potential difference, and that creates a current.}
\begin{align*}
V=RI\,,
\end{align*}
where $R$ is the resistance of the conductor. As we discussed last week, $R$ represents how difficult it is for electrons to flow in a conductor.\\
